Phase 2.2 | Smoothness

p-Refinement:
Degree Elevation

Achieving higher-order continuity without adding extra mesh elements.

1. What is p-Refinement?

In classical FEM, "p-refinement" means increasing the order of the polynomial shape functions in an element. In NURBS, it means elevating the degree ($p$ or $q$) while ensuring the geometry remains exactly invariant.

When you elevate the degree, the knot vector multiplicity increases at the endpoints, and new control points are interpolated between the old ones.

2. The Continuity Advantage

A NURBS basis of degree $p$ is $C^{p-1}$ continuous. Elevating the degree automatically increases the mathematical "smoothness" of the entire patch, making it ideal for high-precision mechanics (like shell theory).
