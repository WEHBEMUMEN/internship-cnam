h-Refinement:
Knot Insertion

Localized subdivision for complex, detailed surfaces.

1. Localized Subdivision

Unlike traditional mesh mesh subdivision (like Catmull-Clark), h-refinement in NURBS works by inserting a new knot into the parametric space. If you insert a knot $\bar{u}$ into the interval $[u_k, u_{k+1}]$, you create a new control point without changing the shape of the surface.

This is mathematically exact subdivision. The new control points are linear combinations of the old ones:

$$\mathbf{P}_i^w = \alpha_i \mathbf{P}_i^w + (1 - \alpha_i) \mathbf{P}_{i-1}^w$$ $$\alpha_i = \frac{\bar{u} - u_i}{u_{i+p} - u_i}$$

2. Why h-Refinement?

In structural analysis, you often need a denser mesh in areas of high stress (concentration). h-refinement allows you to "zooom in" on details by adding control points precisely where they are needed.
