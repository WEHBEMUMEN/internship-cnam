1. k-Refinement: The IGA Advantage

In NURBS, the order of refinement matters. If you perform p-refinement (degree elevation) before h-refinement (knot insertion), you achieve a special state called k-refinement.

This results in a basis that is $C^{p-1}$ continuous across the entire mesh. Traditional Finite Elements are typically only $C^0$ between elements. k-refinement creates the smoothest possible mathematical grid.

2. Designing the Perfect Sphere

A circle cannot be perfectly represented by polynomials, but it can be exactly represented by rational polynomials (NURBS). By using quadratic basis ($p=q=2$) and rational weights of $w = 1/\sqrt{2}$ at the "corners," we create perfectly circular arcs.

Combining these arcs via tensor product yields an Exact NURBS Sphere. This sphere stays mathematically round, even if you zoom in millions of times.
