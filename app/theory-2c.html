The "Offline Phase": Automating the generation of training data for ROM.

1. The Offline-Online Paradigm

Reduced Order Modeling (ROM) splits the computational burden into two distinct phases:

  • Offline Phase (High Cost): We run the full-order nonlinear solver many times for different parameters ($\mu$) and save the resulting displacement fields as **snapshots**.
  • Online Phase (Nearly Free): We use the snapshots to build a compact basis. Solving for a new parameter now takes milliseconds instead of minutes.

2. The Snapshot Matrix

A "Snapshot" is simply the solution vector $u$ for a given combination of material and loading parameters $\mu$. We collect $N$ such snapshots into a large matrix $\mathbf{S}$:

$$\mathbf{S} = \begin{bmatrix} \vert & \vert & & \vert \\ \mathbf{u}(\mu_1) & \mathbf{u}(\mu_2) & \dots & \mathbf{u}(\mu_N) \\ \vert & \vert & & \vert \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{nDofs \times N}$$

This matrix contains all the "physics" of the system. In Phase 2D, we will apply **SVD** to this matrix to find the most dominant modes of deformation.

3. Latin Hypercube Sampling (LHS)

To capture the behavior of the system efficiently, we must choose our parameters $\mu_i$ strategically. Simple random sampling (Monte Carlo) often leads to "clusters" and "Gaps" in the parameter space.

**LHS** ensures uniform coverage by dividing each parameter range into $N$ bins and ensuring each bin is sampled exactly once. This is known as a "Space-Filling" design, which significantly reduces the number of snapshots needed for a high-accuracy ROM.
