Structural Dynamics & Validation

The Mathematical Foundation of the Phase 4.2 Turek CSM Benchmark Suite

1. Objective

Phase 4.2 focuses on the rigorous numerical verification of the Nonlinear Isogeometric Analysis (IGA) engine. By implementing the internationally recognized Turek-Hron CSM Benchmark, we validate the complete computational pipeline, including consistent mass matrix assembly, nonlinear internal force vectors, and second-order temporal integration.

2. Hyperelastic Constitutive Model

We employ the St. Venant-Kirchhoff model, which extends the linear elastic material law to the large-deformation regime using the Green-Lagrange strain tensor \(\mathbf{E}\):

\[ \mathbf{E} = \frac{1}{2} (\mathbf{F}^T \mathbf{F} - \mathbf{I}) = \frac{1}{2} (\nabla \mathbf{u} + \nabla \mathbf{u}^T + \nabla \mathbf{u}^T \nabla \mathbf{u}) \]

The energy density is a quadratic function of the strain, leading to the Second Piola-Kirchhoff stress \(\mathbf{S}\):

\[ \mathbf{S} = \lambda \text{tr}(\mathbf{E})\mathbf{I} + 2\mu\mathbf{E} \]

where \(\lambda\) and \(\mu\) are the Lamé parameters, derived from Young's Modulus \(E\) and Poisson's ratio \(\nu\). This model is particularly effective for verifying geometric nonlinearities in structural benchmarks.

3. Temporal Integration: Newmark-\(\beta\)

To solve the semi-discrete equations of motion \( \mathbf{M}\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{C}\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{F}_{int}(\mathbf{u}) = \mathbf{F}_{ext} \), we utilize the implicit Newmark-\(\beta\) scheme. We specifically use the Average Acceleration Method:

\[ \mathbf{u}_{n+1} = \mathbf{u}_n + \Delta t \mathbf{v}_n + \Delta t^2 \left[ \left(\frac{1}{2} - \beta\right) \mathbf{a}_n + \beta \mathbf{a}_{n+1} \right] \] \[ \mathbf{v}_{n+1} = \mathbf{v}_n + \Delta t \left[ (1 - \gamma) \mathbf{a}_n + \gamma \mathbf{a}_{n+1} \right] \]

With parameters \(\beta = 0.25\) and \(\gamma = 0.5\), the method is second-order accurate and unconditionally stable for linear systems, providing a robust baseline for nonlinear oscillations.
