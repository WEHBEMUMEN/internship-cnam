CSM Benchmark Theory

Numerical Verification of Nonlinear Isogeometric Analysis (IGA)

Objective

The primary objective of Phase 4.2 is to verify the mathematical correctness, numerical stability, and convergence properties of the Nonlinear IGA solver. By comparing the computed results against high-precision reference solutions from the established CSM (Computational Structural Mechanics) benchmark, we ensure that the internal force assembly, tangent stiffness derivation, and time-integration schemes are physically consistent and accurate.

Benchmark Definition

The test case consists of a cantilever beam with length \(L = 0.35\) m and height \(H = 0.02\) m, clamped on the left side. The material follows a St. Venant-Kirchhoff nonlinear hyperelastic model. The loading is purely gravitational, modeled as a constant body force density \( \mathbf{b} = (0, \rho g)^T \).

Reference: Turek, S., & Hron, J. (2006). "Proposal for numerical benchmarking of fluid-structure interaction between an elastic object and laminar incompressible flow." Fluid-Structure Interaction, LNCSE 53, Springer, pp. 371-385.

Body Force Integration

The equivalent nodal forces for gravity are calculated by integrating the shape functions over the physical volume (or area in 2D): \[ \mathbf{F}_i^{body} = \int_{\Omega} N_i(u,v) \cdot \rho \cdot \mathbf{g} \cdot \text{det}(\mathbf{J}) \cdot t \, du \, dv \] Where \(N_i\) are the NURBS basis functions, \(\rho\) is the material density, and \(t\) is the thickness. This phase verifies that our quadrature rules correctly capture the full mass of the structure.
