Phase 3.4b | Unassembled Hyper-Reduction

U-DEIM Theory

Eliminating the Global Assembly Bottleneck via Element-Level Sampling.

The Motivation

1. Beyond Global DEIM

In standard DEIM (Phase 3.4a), we approximate the global internal force vector $\mathbf{F}_{int} \in \mathbb{R}^N$. To compute even a few entries of this vector at the online stage, we often have to evaluate many elements. If a selected DOF index belongs to multiple elements (common in IGA due to high continuity), we must compute the local force for all those elements and assemble them.

U-DEIM (Unassembled DEIM) solves this by approximating the forces before they are assembled into the global vector.

The Mathematical Foundation

2. The Unassembled Formulation

The total internal force is a sum of element contributions:

$$\mathbf{F}_{int}(\mathbf{u}) = \sum_{e=1}^{n_e} \mathbf{L}_e^T \mathbf{f}_e(\mathbf{u}_e)$$

We define the unassembled force vector $\mathcal{F}$ as a long vector containing all local element forces stacked together:

$$\mathcal{F} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{f}_1 \\ \mathbf{f}_2 \\ \vdots \\ \mathbf{f}_{n_e} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{\mathcal{N}}, \quad \mathcal{N} = n_e \times n_{dof}^{elem}$$

U-DEIM approximates $\mathcal{F}$ using a POD basis $\mathbf{U}_{\mathcal{F}}$ and sampling a subset of element-level DOFs indices $\mathcal{P}$:

$$\mathcal{F} \approx \mathbf{U}_{\mathcal{F}} (\mathbf{P}^T \mathbf{U}_{\mathcal{F}})^{-1} \mathbf{P}^T \mathcal{F}$$
The Offline Phase

3. Training U-DEIM

The training process involves three key steps:

  1. Snapshot Collection: For each training load, we record the local force vector of every element separately.
  2. SVD: We perform Singular Value Decomposition on the unassembled snapshot matrix to get the force basis $\mathbf{U}_{\mathcal{F}}$.
  3. Greedy Selection: We use the standard DEIM greedy algorithm, but it now picks indices in the unassembled space. This effectively selects which elements and which of their local DOFs are most representative of the total nonlinear work.
Online Efficiency

4. Online Speedup

The real power of U-DEIM is seen during the online solve. To compute the reduced force $\mathbf{F}_r = \Phi^T \mathbf{F}_{int}$, we only need to:

  • Evaluate $\mathbf{f}_e$ for the few elements selected by $\mathbf{P}$.
  • Multiply by the precomputed matrix $\Phi^T \mathbf{A} \mathbf{U}_{\mathcal{F}} (\mathbf{P}^T \mathbf{U}_{\mathcal{F}})^{-1}$ (where $\mathbf{A}$ is the assembly operator).

Since we only evaluate a handful of elements, the cost becomes independent of the mesh size $N$, achieving true Hyper-Reduction.
