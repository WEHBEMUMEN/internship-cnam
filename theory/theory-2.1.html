1. The Tensor Product Mesh

A NURBS surface is formed by the Tensor Product of two univariate basis systems. If you have basis functions $N_{i,p}(\xi)$ in the longitudinal direction and $M_{j,q}(\eta)$ in the lateral direction, the bivariate basis is:

$$R_{i,j}(\xi, \eta) = \frac{N_{i,p}(\xi) M_{j,q}(\eta) w_{i,j}}{\sum \sum N_{k,p}(\xi) M_{l,q}(\eta) w_{k,l}}$$

This creates a grid-like influence where each control point $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}$ affects a local rectangular patch of the surface.
