Theory & Physics

Documenting the transition from infinitesimal elastic theory to finite-strain geometric stiffening.

1. Finite Kinematics

In linear elasticity, we assume rotations are tiny ($\cos\theta \approx 1$). In Geometric Nonlinearity, we throw that assumption away. We track the deformation from the reference state $\mathbf{X}$ to the current state $\mathbf{x}$.

Deformation Gradient

$$\mathbf{F} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{x}}{\partial \mathbf{X}} = \mathbf{I} + \nabla_{\mathbf{X}} \mathbf{u}$$

To measure true structural stretching without picking up "fake" strains from pure rotation, we use the Green-Lagrange Strain Tensor $\mathbf{E}$:

$$\mathbf{E} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{F}^T\mathbf{F} - \mathbf{I}) = \frac{1}{2}(\nabla\mathbf{u} + \nabla\mathbf{u}^T + \nabla\mathbf{u}^T\nabla\mathbf{u})$$

Notice the quadratic term $\nabla\mathbf{u}^T\nabla\mathbf{u}$. This is the "Engine" of nonlinearity. Even if your displacement is linear, this term generates the geometric stiffening that prevents cantilever beams from stretching into infinity.

2. Solver Pipeline

Since the stiffness depends on the displacement, we cannot solve it in one shot. We use the Newton-Raphson algorithm to find the equilibrium where internal stress exactly balances the external load.

3. Penalty Constraint (Symmetry Fixing)

In geometries like the Plate with a Hole, the NURBS mapping becomes "collapsed" at the center diagonal. Multiple control points occupy the same physical coordinate to form the circular boundary.

The Problem: Rank Deficiency

Without tying these points, the stiffness matrix becomes singular. The solver tries to move coincident points independently, causing the numerical stress to explode ("flare") at the singular center.

Our solver implements a Penalty Energy term to tie these coincident Degrees of Freedom (DOFs):

$$\Pi_{penalty} = \frac{1}{2} \beta |u_i - u_j|^2$$

By using a massive penalty factor ($\beta \approx 10^{12}$), we effectively glue those nodes together, ensuring smooth stress gradients transitions across the symmetry diagonal.

4. Macroscopic Analysis

The simulator provides two critical real-time graphs to validate the nonlinear physics:

Force-Displacement
