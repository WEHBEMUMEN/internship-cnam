Galerkin Projection &
Reduced Operators

Transforming the physics of continuum elements into the POD subspace.

1. Projecting the System Equations

The Full Order Model (FOM) requires solving a large system of linear equations:

$$\mathbf{K}_{fom} \mathbf{u} = \mathbf{F}_{fom}$$

By substituting the POD approximation $\mathbf{u} \approx \mathbf{\Phi} \mathbf{q}$ and applying the **Galerkin condition** (the residual must be orthogonal to the basis), we project the equations into the reduced subspace:

$$\mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{K}_{fom} \mathbf{\Phi} \mathbf{q} = \mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{F}_{fom}$$

2. The Reduced Stiffness Matrix

The **Reduced Stiffness Matrix** $\mathbf{K}_r$ is a tiny $k \times k$ matrix ($k \approx 5$) that represents the energy distribution across the dominant modes:

$$\mathbf{K}_r = \mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{K}_{fom} \mathbf{\Phi} \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$$

Because $\mathbf{\Phi}$ is orthonormal, this projection is mathematically stable. $\mathbf{K}_r$ remains symmetric and positive definite (SPD), ensuring a unique and stable structural solution.

3. Scaling Efficiency

The computational complexity of solving the system in the Online phase changes dramatically:

FOM Solver

Solving for $N$ degrees of freedom.

$O(N^3)$

Takes seconds to minutes for large meshes.

ROM Solver

Solving for $k$ modal coefficients.

$O(k^3)$

Takes < 1ms, allowing interactive dynamics.

4. The Offline/Online Split

The beauty of the Galerkin Projection is that for linear systems, $\mathbf{K}_r$ is independent of the load magnitude. We compute it once (Offline) and use it repeatedly for different real-time queries (Online):

  • Offline: Assemble $\mathbf{K}_{fom}$ and pre-compute $\mathbf{K}_r = \mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{K}_{fom} \mathbf{\Phi}$.
  • Online: Assemble only the reduced force $\mathbf{F}_r = \mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{F}_{fom}$ and solve $\mathbf{K}_r \mathbf{q} = \mathbf{F}_r$.
