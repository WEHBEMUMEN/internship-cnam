Phase 5.2: Geometric Defect ROM

Parametrizing Material Loss and Structural Failure in Cantilever Beams

Objective: To simulate the structural response of a cantilever beam with a variable geometric defect. The parameters $\mathbf{\mu} = [\text{pos}, \text{depth}]$ define the location and severity of the material loss. ECSW is used to maintain real-time performance across the entire defect parameter space.

1. Defect Parametrization

The geometry is defined by a mapping $\mathbf{x}(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\mu})$ where $\mathbf{\mu} = [\mu_{pos}, r_{depth}]$. The control points $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}$ are displaced at the notch location to represent the material loss:

$$\mathbf{P}_{i,j}(\mathbf{\mu}) = \begin{cases} \mathbf{P}_{i,j}^0 - r \cdot \mathbf{n} & \text{if } x_i \approx \mu_{pos} \text{ and } j \in \{0, n_v\} \\ \mathbf{P}_{i,j}^0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

This parametrization allows the Digital Twin to explore "What-if" failure scenarios by moving the defect along the beam span.

2. Hyper-reduced Assembly

Because the mesh must be refined around the notch to capture stress concentrations, the number of elements $N_e$ increases. ECSW ensures that the assembly cost remains constant:

$$\mathbf{k}_r(\mathbf{\mu}) \approx \sum_{e \in E^*} \xi_e \left( \mathbf{\Phi}_e^T \mathbf{k}_e(\mathbf{\mu}) \mathbf{\Phi}_e \right)$$

where $\xi_e > 0$ are the non-negative weights and $\mathbf{\Phi}_e$ is the basis restricted to element $e$.

3. Offline Training: Sparse Optimization

The weights are found by solving a Non-Negative Least Squares (NNLS) problem that ensures the reduced internal force is preserved for all training snapshots.

Algorithm 1: ECSW Training (Greedy NNLS)
1. Assemble G-Matrix: For each training snapshot $s$ and each element $e$:
   $\mathbf{G}_{s,e} = \text{vec}(\mathbf{\Phi}_e^T \mathbf{f}_{int,e}(u_s))$
2. Target Vector b: $\mathbf{b}_s = \text{vec}(\mathbf{\Phi}^T \mathbf{f}_{int,full}(u_s))$
3. Solve NNLS: $\min_{\mathbf{w} \geq 0} \|\mathbf{G}\mathbf{w} - \mathbf{b}\|_2$
4. Sparsify: Select elements where $w_e > 0$.
5. Output: Sampled indices $E^*$ and weights $\xi_e$.

4. Online Hyper-reduction

In the online phase, the solver only visits the elements in $E^*$. For a plate with 10,000 elements, $E^*$ might only contain 100 elements, leading to a theoretical speedup of $100\times$.

Algorithm 2: Online ECSW Solve
1. Input: New shape $\mathbf{\theta}$, Weights $\mathbf{\xi}$, Basis $\mathbf{\Phi}$.
2. Sparse Assembly:
   $\mathbf{k}_r = \mathbf{0}_{k \times k}$
   For $e \in E^*$:
      a. Compute $\mathbf{k}_e(\mathbf{\theta})$ for the morphed element.
      b. Accumulate: $\mathbf{k}_r += \xi_e (\mathbf{\Phi}_e^T \mathbf{k}_e \mathbf{\Phi}_e)$.
3. Reduced Solve: $\mathbf{k}_r \mathbf{a} = \mathbf{f}_r$.
4. Output: Instantaneous result.
