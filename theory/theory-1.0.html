Phase 1.0: Resolution & Performance

Understanding the computational cost of continuous smoothness.

The Evaluation Loop

In Isogeometric Analysis (IGA), we evaluate a curve $\mathbf{C}(\xi)$ by summing the product of control points $\mathbf{P}_i$ and their respective basis functions $R_{i,p}(\xi)$:

\mathbf{C}(\xi) = \sum_{i=0}^{n} R_{i,p}(\xi) \mathbf{P}_i

To render this on a screen, we must discretize the continuous parameter $\xi \in [0, 1]$ into $N$ discrete evaluation points. This is where the trade-off begins.

Computational Complexity

The time complexity for a single frame is $O(N \cdot p^2)$, where:

  • $N$ is the number of evaluation points (Resolution).
  • $p$ is the polynomial degree.

Because the Cox-de Boor recursion is applied for every point, increasing the resolution from 500 to 5000 increases the CPU load by 10x. If $p$ increases, the cost grows quadratically per point.

Visual Smoothness

Why do we need more points? For a standard screen, 500 points usually suffice for a "smooth" appearance. However:

  • Zooming: When zooming in on a small segment of the curve, 500 points across the whole domain might lead to visible "segments".
  • Curvature: High-curvature regions (sharp turns) require denser sampling to avoid aliasing.
