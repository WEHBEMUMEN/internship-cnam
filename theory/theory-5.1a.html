Phase 5.1a: Shape-Parametric Reduced-Order Modeling

In Phase 5, we transition from Force-Parametric ROM to Geometric-Parametric ROM. In this regime, the physical domain $\Omega$ is no longer static; its boundary $\Gamma$ and internal metric $g$ are functions of a shape parameter vector $\mathbf{\theta}$. This is the foundation of Geometric Digital Twins.

1. NURBS Geometric Morphing

A NURBS surface is defined by its control points $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}$. To enable parametric geometry, we define a mapping function $\mathcal{M}$ that evolves these points based on the shape vector $\mathbf{\theta} = [R, L_1, L_2]^T$:

$$\mathbf{P}_{i,j}(\mathbf{\theta}) = \mathcal{M}(\mathbf{P}_{i,j}^{ref}, \mathbf{\theta})$$

For the notched plate, the mapping for the inner boundary ($R$) and outer dimensions ($L_1, L_2$) must maintain the exact circularity of the hole while stretching the surrounding "tensor-mesh." This is achieved by a linear blending between the radial notch coordinates and the rectangular outer boundary coordinates.

2. Parametric Jacobian and Stiffness

In force-parametric ROM, the stiffness matrix $\mathbf{K}$ is constant. In geometric ROM, every change in $\mathbf{\theta}$ alters the Jacobian $\mathbf{J}(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta})$ of the mapping from the reference unit square to the physical space. The parametric stiffness is defined as:

$$\mathbf{K}(\mathbf{\theta}) = \int_{\hat{\Omega}} \mathbf{B}^T(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta}) \mathbf{D} \mathbf{B}(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta}) \det(\mathbf{J}(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta})) d\hat{\Omega}$$

Because $\mathbf{K}$ is now a function of $\mathbf{\theta}$, the snapshot collection process becomes computationally intensive, as it requires a full assembly and solve for each geometric sample. The goal of Phase 5 is to build a reduced basis $\mathbf{\Phi}$ that can approximate the displacement field $\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{\theta})$ even as the integration domain itself deforms.

3. Linear Static Benchmark: The Kirsch Problem

We utilize the Plate with a Hole (Notch) benchmark. Due to symmetry, we solve a quarter-model with the following boundary conditions:

  • Bottom Edge ($y=0$): $u_y = 0$ (Symmetry)
  • Left Edge ($x=0$): $u_x = 0$ (Symmetry)
  • Right Edge ($x=L_1$): $\sigma_{xx} = T_x$ (Tension)

The exact stress concentration factor (SCF) at the notch is theoretically $3.0$ for an infinite plate. In our Digital Twin, the ROM must capture the localized stress gradients as $R$ increases relative to $L_1$ and $L_2$, demonstrating Topological Sensitivity.

4. Multidimensional Shape-Space Sweep

The offline trainer performs a grid search across the parameters:

$$\mathcal{D}_{\theta} = \{ (R_i, L1_j, L2_k) \}$$

For each triplet, a linear static IGA solution is obtained. The resulting snapshot matrix $\mathbf{S}$ is then decomposed via SVD to extract the dominant geometric modes that represent the most common deformation patterns across the shape-space.
