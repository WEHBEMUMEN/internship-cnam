Phase 5.1a: Shape-Parametric Reduced-Order Modeling

In Phase 5, we transition from Force-Parametric ROM to Geometric-Parametric ROM. In this regime, the physical domain $\Omega$ is no longer static; its boundary $\Gamma$ and internal metric $g$ are functions of a shape parameter vector $\mathbf{\theta} = [R, L_1, L_2]^T$. This shift represents the core challenge in building Geometric Digital Twins, where the ROM must adapt to topological and dimensional variations.

1. Isogeometric Analysis (IGA) Foundations

Isogeometric Analysis utilizes the same basis functions for both the geometry representation and the approximation of the solution field. For a bivariate NURBS surface, the geometry is mapped from the parametric space $(\xi, \eta)$ to the physical space via:

$$\mathbf{x}(\xi, \eta) = \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^m R_{i,j}^{p,q}(\xi, \eta) \mathbf{P}_{i,j}$$

where $R_{i,j}^{p,q}$ are the rational basis functions of degree $p$ and $q$, and $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}$ are the control points. In Phase 5.1a, we treat these control points as functions of the shape parameters $\mathbf{\theta}$.

2. NURBS Geometric Morphing Mapping

To enable parametric geometry without manual remeshing, we define a continuous mapping function $\mathcal{M}$ that evolves the reference control points $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}^{ref}$ based on the shape vector $\mathbf{\theta}$:

$$\mathbf{P}_{i,j}(\mathbf{\theta}) = \mathcal{M}(\mathbf{P}_{i,j}^{ref}, \mathbf{\theta})$$

For the notched plate quarter-model, we define a radial-to-rectangular blending. The inner control points are constrained to an arc of radius $R$, while the outer points define a rectangle of size $L_1 \times L_2$. The intermediate points are computed using a linear blend to ensure a smooth "tensor-mesh" distribution that avoids element inversion (Jacobian positivity):

$$\mathbf{P}_{mid}(\mathbf{\theta}) = (1 - \alpha) \mathbf{P}_{inner}(R) + \alpha \mathbf{P}_{outer}(L_1, L_2)$$

3. Parametric Stiffness and Jacobian Sensitivity

In geometric ROM, the stiffness matrix $\mathbf{K}$ depends on the shape $\mathbf{\theta}$ through the Jacobian $\mathbf{J}$ and the strain-displacement matrix $\mathbf{B}$:

$$\mathbf{K}(\mathbf{\theta}) = \int_{\hat{\Omega}} \mathbf{B}^T(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta}) \mathbf{D} \mathbf{B}(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta}) |\mathbf{J}(\xi, \eta; \mathbf{\theta})| d\xi d\eta$$

The Jacobian $\mathbf{J}$ represents the metric of the transformation. As the notch radius $R$ grows, the elements near the hole compress, increasing the local stiffness and altering the stress concentration patterns. The ROM must capture these metric sensitivities without re-assembling the full-order model (FOM) during online evaluation.

4. The Snapshot Proper Orthogonal Decomposition (POD)

The offline trainer constructs a reduced basis $\mathbf{\Phi}$ by harvesting solutions (snapshots) across a discretized shape-space $\mathcal{D}_{\theta}$:

  1. Sampling: Generate $N_s$ snapshots $\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{\theta}_i)$ for $i=1 \dots N_s$.
  2. Snapshot Matrix: Construct $\mathbf{S} = [\mathbf{u}(\mathbf{\theta}_1), \mathbf{u}(\mathbf{\theta}_2), \dots, \mathbf{u}(\mathbf{\theta}_{N_s})]$.
  3. SVD: Perform Singular Value Decomposition $\mathbf{S} = \mathbf{U}\mathbf{\Sigma}\mathbf{V}^T$.
  4. Truncation: Select the first $k$ columns of $\mathbf{U}$ to form the basis $\mathbf{\Phi} \in \mathbb{R}^{N_{dof} \times k}$.

The energy captured by the $k$ modes is defined by the singular values $\sigma_i$:

$$\mathcal{E}(k) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^k \sigma_i^2}{\sum_{j=1}^{N_s} \sigma_j^2}$$

5. Numerical Benchmark: The Kirsch Solution

The Plate with a Hole benchmark is used to validate the ROM. For an infinite plate under tension $T$, the radial stress $\sigma_{rr}$ and tangential stress $\sigma_{\theta\theta}$ are given by:

$$\sigma_{\theta\theta} = \frac{T}{2} \left( 1 + \frac{R^2}{r^2} \right) - \frac{T}{2} \left( 1 + 3\frac{R^4}{r^4} \right) \cos(2\theta)$$

At $r=R$ and $\theta=\pi/2$, $\sigma_{\theta\theta} = 3T$. Our ROM targets this localized gradient, ensuring that the Geometric Digital Twin remains accurate even under significant shape distortions.

6. Nomenclature and Terminology

  • $\Omega, \Gamma$: The physical domain (the plate) and its boundary.
  • $\mathbf{\theta} = [R, L_1, L_2]^T$: The shape parameter vector containing the Notch Radius, Plate Width, and Plate Height.
  • $R_{i,j}^{p,q}(\xi, \eta)$: Bivariate NURBS basis functions of degree $p$ in $\xi$ and $q$ in $\eta$.
  • $\mathbf{P}_{i,j}$: Control points that define the skeleton of the geometric shape.
  • $\mathcal{M}(\cdot)$: The morphing operator that translates parameter changes into control point displacements.
  • $\mathbf{J} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{x}}{\partial \mathbf{\xi}}$: The Jacobian matrix representing the local transformation from parametric to physical space.
  • $|\mathbf{J}|$: The determinant of the Jacobian, representing the local area (or volume) change.
  • $\mathbf{B}(\mathbf{\theta})$: The strain-displacement matrix, which now varies with the geometry.
  • $\mathbf{K}(\mathbf{\theta})$: The global stiffness matrix, assembled by integrating the material properties over the deformed domain.
  • $\mathbf{S} \in \mathbb{R}^{N_{dof} \times N_s}$: The snapshot matrix containing full-order solutions for different shapes.
  • $\mathbf{\Phi}$: The POD basis (Reduced Basis), a set of orthogonal vectors that best span the snapshot space.
  • $\sigma_i$: Singular values indicating the relative "importance" or energy contribution of each mode.
