Phase 3.3 | Periodic Benchmarking

Annulus Twisting &
Follower Forces

Testing the limits of geometric nonlinearity with periodic topologies and displacement-dependent loads.

1. Periodic Boundary Conditions

To simulate a closed annulus (a ring), we use a single NURBS patch where the start edge and end edge occupy the same physical space. However, in the stiffness matrix, they are independent degrees of freedom.

We enforce Periodicity using the Penalty Method. We identify coincident control points at the boundary and "glue" them together by adding a massive stiffness term between them:

Periodic Coupling

$$\Pi_{period} = \frac{1}{2} \beta \sum (u_{start} - u_{end})^2$$

This ensures that as the annulus twists, the displacement and stress fields remain perfectly continuous across the "invisible" seam.

2. Follower Force Logic

In standard structural mechanics, loads are often fixed in space (e.g., gravity). However, in many Digital Twin applications (like pressure in a tank or torque on a shaft), the load vector rotates with the material.

Follower Traction

$$\mathbf{f}(\mathbf{u}) = t \cdot \mathbf{n}(\mathbf{u})$$

The load vector is updated in every iteration of the Newton-Raphson loop to stay tangential to the deformed boundary. This creates an additional source of nonlinearity, as the external force $\mathbf{F}_{ext}$ is no longer constant.

3. Large Rotation Stability

Phase 3.3 serves as a stress-test for our solver's stability. By applying a large follower torque to the annulus, we can observe rotations exceeding $180^\circ$. This validates:

  • The robustness of the Green-Lagrange strain tensor under extreme rotation.
  • The convergence of the Newton-Raphson loop with dynamic external loads.
  • The visual accuracy of our Three.js deformation pipeline.

4. Analytical Verification

To ensure the numerical stability of the 2D engine, we cross-validate results against Lamé's Torsion Solution at low load increments. While the nonlinear response will deviate (capturing the Poynting effect), the linear baseline provides a perfect sanity check for boundary conditions and nodal assembly.
