METHODOLOGY

Mathematical Smoothness

Traditional computer models use simple straight lines and flat triangles to represent the world. In the physical world, however, stresses and strains are smooth and continuous. Our project uses the Isogeometric Analysis (IGA) paradigm to bridge this gap.

The Chain vs. Silk Thread

Standard engineering models (FEA) are like a chain of metal links. They are strong, but at every "link" there is a sharp corner where forces can get stuck. IGA is like a single, unbreakable silk thread. Because there are no links or sharp corners, the mathematical "force" can flow smoothly from one end to the other without ever getting trapped or producing errors.

1. What is $C^{p-1}$ Continuity?

In mathematics, we measure "smoothness" by how many derivatives a function has. If a curve is $C^0$, it is connected. If it is $C^1$, its slope is smooth. If it is $C^2$, its curvature (bending) is smooth.

B-Splines of degree $p$ naturally provide $C^{p-1}$ continuity across elements. This means a cubic B-Spline ($p=3$) is perfectly smooth even in its bending behavior.

Structural Integrity

Because the math is smooth, we don't need "fine meshes" to get accurate stress values at the junctions. One big element works as well as 100 small ones.

Design Fidelity

Our simulation uses the SAME mathematics as the designer's original CAD model. What you see is exactly what the engine solves.

2. Overlapping Influence

NURBS basis functions have a unique "overlapping" property. Each control point affects a local neighborhood, but its influence fades gracefully. This overlap is what creates the "smoothness" and ensures that if you pull on one point, the whole beam responds organically, not just the nearest section.
