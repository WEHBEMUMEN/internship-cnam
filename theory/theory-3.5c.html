Phase 3.5c | Energy-Conserving Hyper-Reduction

ECSW Theory

Stability-preserving element sampling for nonlinear structural dynamics.

The Stability Challenge

1. Why ECSW?

While DEIM (Phase 3.4a) is mathematically elegant, it does not guarantee the stability of the reduced-order model. In nonlinear dynamics, small interpolation errors in DEIM can lead to artificial energy growth, causing the simulation to diverge.

ECSW (Energy Conserving Sampling and Weighting), proposed by Farhat et al., addresses this by ensuring that the reduced internal force vector preserves the virtual work (energy) of the full system.

The Formulation

2. Virtual Work Approximation

The goal is to approximate the projected internal force $\mathbf{f}_r = \Phi^T \mathbf{f}_{int}$ as a weighted sum of a few element contributions:

$$\Phi^T \mathbf{f}_{int}(\mathbf{u}) \approx \sum_{e \in \mathcal{E}} w_e \Phi_e^T \mathbf{f}_e(\mathbf{u}_e)$$

where $\mathcal{E}$ is a small subset of elements, and $w_e > 0$ are positive weights. The weights are calculated such that the approximation is accurate for all snapshots in the training set.

NNLS Training

3. Non-Negative Least Squares

The weights $w_e$ are found by solving a constrained optimization problem:

$$\min_{\mathbf{w} \geq 0} \|\mathbf{G} \mathbf{w} - \mathbf{b}\|_2^2$$

where $\mathbf{G}$ contains the projected element forces for all snapshots, and $\mathbf{b}$ contains the projected full internal forces. By enforcing $\mathbf{w} \geq 0$, ECSW ensures that the reduced tangent stiffness matrix remains positive definite, preserving the **stability** of the nonlinear solver.
